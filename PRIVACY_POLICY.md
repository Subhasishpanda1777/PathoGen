## 1. Introduction

Welcome to PathoGen, a public health monitoring platform designed to track disease outbreaks, suggest affordable medicine alternatives, and promote community health in India. PathoGen ("we," "us," "our," or "Platform") is committed to protecting your privacy and personal data.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, protect, and share your personal information when you use our Platform. This policy complies with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act) of India and other applicable data protection laws.

By using PathoGen, you consent to the practices described in this Privacy Policy.

We collect the following personal information that you provide to us:

- **Email Address**: Required for account creation and communication

## 6. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your personal information:

### 6.1 Encryption
- **AES-256-GCM Encryption**: All personally identifiable information (PII) is encrypted at rest
- **HTTPS/TLS**: All data transmission is encrypted in transit
- **Secure Keys**: Encryption keys stored securely in environment variables

### 6.2 Access Controls
- **Authentication**: Multi-factor authentication (OTP + Password)
- **Authorization**: Role-based access control (users, admins)
- **Limited Access**: Only authorized personnel can access your data

### 6.3 Technical Safeguards
- **Password Hashing**: Passwords encrypted using bcrypt (industry standard)
- **Input Validation**: All inputs validated to prevent security vulnerabilities
- **SQL Injection Protection**: Parameterized queries prevent database attacks
- **XSS Protection**: Content sanitization prevents cross-site scripting

### 6.4 Security Monitoring
- **Error Monitoring**: Sentry for tracking security issues
- **Logging**: Comprehensive logging for security audit trails
- **Regular Reviews**: Periodic security assessments

**However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.**

## 7. Data Retention

### 7.1 Retention Periods

- **Account Data**: Retained while your account is active, deleted upon account deletion
- **Symptom Reports**: Retained for public health purposes (typically 2-5 years), then anonymized
- **Health Risk Scores**: Retained for 1 year, then deleted
- **Logs**: Retained for 90 days for security and debugging purposes

### 7.2 Deletion

- **Upon Request**: You can request deletion of your data at any time
- **Account Deletion**: Deleting your account will remove all personal data
- **Anonymization**: Historical data may be anonymized for public health research

## 8. Your Rights (DPDP Act 2023)

Under the DPDP Act and applicable laws, you have the following rights:

### 8.1 Right to Access
- **Request Access**: You can request a copy of your personal data
- **Implementation**: Available through your account dashboard or API endpoint

### 8.2 Right to Correction
- **Update Information**: You can correct inaccurate or incomplete data
- **Implementation**: Update your profile through the platform

### 8.3 Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten)
- **Delete Account**: You can request deletion of your account and personal data
- **Implementation**: Contact support or use account deletion feature

### 8.4 Right to Data Portability
- **Export Data**: You can request your data in a portable format (JSON/CSV)
- **Implementation**: Data export feature (coming soon)

### 8.5 Right to Withdraw Consent
- **Opt-Out**: You can withdraw consent for data processing
- **Implementation**: Update privacy settings in your account

### 8.6 Right to Grievance Redressal
- **Complaints**: You can file complaints about data processing
- **Contact**: Email privacy@pathogen.in or use the grievance form

### How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, please:
1. **Email**: privacy@pathogen.in
2. **Account Settings**: Use in-platform privacy controls
3. **Support**: Contact our support team

We will respond to your request within 30 days as required by law.
